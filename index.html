About

From Rio de Janeiro
to Canada's Mats

Jorge Britto Lima Filho is a Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu black belt under Saulo Ribeiro and Vini Aieta, world champion competitor, coach, and founder of the Jiu-Jitsu For Life Team — one of Canada's most respected BJJ associations.

Born on December 13, 1979 in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, Jorge discovered jiu-jitsu at 13 years old. Training out of the legendary Gracie Tijuca academy — an affiliate of Gracie Humaitá led by Vinicius Aieta and Saulo Ribeiro — he developed alongside future stars like Letícia Ribeiro, Vinny Magalhães, and Alexandre Ribeiro.

Driven by ambition and inspired by the words of Wellington Megaton Dias — "You love jiu-jitsu and want to make a living from it, learn two things: English and how to teach a class. The future is abroad" — Jorge dedicated himself to building a life through the art.

Promoted to black belt on February 12, 2005, Jorge also ventured into MMA with the sole purpose of testing his grappling to the fullest, finishing with a 12-9-1 record. After his US visa was denied three times, he finally secured a working visa for Canada and moved in May 2008. He went on to form the Jiu-Jitsu For Life Team in October 2016 and founded the Ontario Jiu-Jitsu Championship (OJJC), establishing himself as a cornerstone of Canadian BJJ.

Competition Highlights

  • IBJJF World Masters Champion (2015)
  • IBJJF World No-Gi Champion (2011 / 2009 — Master 1)
  • IBJJF Pans Champion (2016 / 2015 Master 2 · 2009 Master 1)
  • ADCC Europe Champion (2010)
  • ADCC Europe 3rd Place (2012)
  • IBJJF World No-Gi 2nd Place (2009)
  • CBJJO Brazilian National Cup 2nd Place (2006)
